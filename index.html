Ziming Liu

I am an incoming PhD student at MIT, advised by Max Tegmark. I mainly focus on the intersection of artificial intelligence and physics. I am fascinated with both fundamental and practical problems. Fundamental: (1) the nature of intelligence from perspective of physics and biology; (2) learning theory with tools from physics and information theory. Practical: building more powerful, efficient, robust and safe AI systems (for vision, language, self-driving cars, games) that can benefit the well-being of all humans. My CV is here.

Welcome Collaboration!

I am looking forward to working with talented people with diverse backgrounds. Currently I am particularly interested in or working on these topics:

  • (1) Embedding physical inductive biases (e.g, symmetry and conservation law) to machine learning

  • (2) Understanding the benefits (if any) of modularity and hierarchy in neural network architecture from the perspective of physics, information and neuroscience

  • (3) Analysis of deep learning with computational tools from statistical physics, field theory and information theory

  • (4) Symbolic regression and its potential for new scientific discovery

  • (5) General and domain-specific AI systems (vision, language, self-driving cars and games) and their connections
Note: for undergraduate students, you don't have to be an expert in machine learning to collaborate with me, as long as you are eagar to learn and share some of my interests. Here is a great example showing how I cooperate with junior students and we had a lot of fun!

Projects

  • Schrodinger PCA: You only Need Variances for Eigenmodes, arXiv:2002.06061. (Ziming Liu, Sitian Qian, Yixuan Wang, Yuxuan Yan, Tianyi Yang)
    Comment: We make an intriguing connection between quantum mechanics and principal component analysis. Please also check the video here.


  • Quantum-Inspired Hamiltonian Monte Carlo for Bayesian Sampling, arXiv:1912.01937 (Ziming Liu, Zheng Zhang)
    Comment: What will happen when quantum mechanics meets hamiltonian monte carlo? The quantum mass achieves better sampling results on spiky and multi-modal distributions.



  • Principal Component Analysis of Collective Flow in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions, published in European Physical Journal C. (Ziming Liu, Wenbin Zhao and Huichao Song)

    • Comment: why do scientists use Fourier bases to decompose flow data? In this paper, we provide a tentative answer towards this question with the help of principal component analysis (PCA).



  • Influenza Modeling Based on Massive Feature Engineering and International Flow Deconvolution, arXiv: 1912.02989 (Ziming Liu, Yixuan Wang, Zizhao Han, Dian Wu)
    Comment: This project is for the "DataOpen" Competition held by Citadel & Correlation One. We won the champion in the Beijing competition!


  • Robustness of principal component analysis on harmonic flow in heavy ion collisions, arXiv:2002.06061. (Ziming Liu, Arabinda Behera, Huichao Song, Jiangyong Jia)
    Comment: Sometimes experiments are very different from theory, so we need to be really careful! We provide a example that sub-leading flow can be arisen from non-flow effects in experiments.
