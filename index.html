Highlighted Timeline

  • *  (September 2019) I am pleased to participate in the seminar held by Professor's Bin Dong at BICMR and present a talk on Langevin-type sampling methods. Thanks for the discussion !


  • *  (September 2019-) I will hold seminars on Quantum Computing & Quantum information with Zhihan Li and Yixuan Wang from Department of Mathematics. We expect the seminar to be once a week, starting from September 21st, and every Saturday afternoon afterwards, in Room 1570, No.1 Science Building. Detailed schedules, related handouts and materials will come out soon Related materials are available here.

    • *  (July-September 2019)I am happy to have worked in Professor Zheng Zhang's great lab @UCSB ECE. We investigated a lot of numerical methods for high-order tensor learning, and physics-inspired bayesian learning tools. We proposed a novel sampling methods based on Hamiltonian Monte Carlo with improvement inspired from the theory of quantum mechanics. Experimental results shown the proposed method has good experimental results in the applications of image denoising, neural network pruning etc. The paper will be available on arxiv soon.

    *  (July 2019) Coming back to Stony Brook University again and having a great time with SUNY Heavy-Ion Group. I also give a talk on the potential of applying many kinds of PCA-type methods to high energy phenomenological sciences. Autoencoder (kernel PCA) can automatically extract features in the underlying manifold of initial states, and Robust PCA can provide a strong tool to model the difference between collective flow (low rank) and non-flow (sparse). Get the slides here.

    *  (June 2019) Initial Stages 2019 at Columbia Univeristy. I am happy to learn so much about the frontiers of physics. A new interesting trend is the combination of data science and phenomenological sciences, e.g. using a neural network to extract the PDF of the proton, using bayesian methods to estimate uncertainty of shear viscosity, and my talk which focuses on applying PCA to particles data to extract main features in data in an unsupervised way. Get the slides here.

    *  (May 2019) I am honored to have a chance to give a talk online for HENPIC, which is a monthly online conference for High Energy Physics in China, and most of speakers are promising young postdocs or professors. I gave a high-level overview on the application of machine learning in many fields of physics including quantum information, high energy physics, astronomy and condensed matter physics. Get the slides here.

    *  (April 2019) The exciting DataOpen final @NYSE held by Correlation One and sponsored by Citadel ! We represent Peking University to compete with other 23 winner teams from other universities. We aim to investigate the relationship between migration/imports and spread of influenza. We construct an elegant model with the convolutional graph as the main model and utilzed a lot of useful tricks including matrix imputation and autoencoder, etc. Get the paper.

    *  (March 2019) I finalized my first academic paper ! Great thanks to Professor Huichao Song from High Energy Center in Peking University! In this paper, we apply principal component analysis to extract important features in the azimuthal direction in relativistic collisions. Based on the PCA eigenmodes, we propose novel observables, whose correlations has surprisingly simple structures that can challenge a long-held belief about the complexity and non-linearity of relativistic hydrodynamics. Get the paper.


    *  (January 2019) I am happy to have worked in Professor Jiang yong Jia's Heavy-Ion group at SUNY. We investigate to develop data-driven tools that can identify the factorization breaking part of the emitted particles originated from the quark gluon plasma. We also challenge one traditional method and point out the limitations of tradtional approaches and the way out. The paper will be on arxiv soon.

    *  (December 2018) I present a talk on 4th China LHC workshop. The talk is about applying principal component analysis to study phenomenological models in physics. Get the slide here.

    *  (May 2018) We are the winner of DataOpen Marathon in Beijing. The competition is held by Correlation One and sponsored by Citadel. Great thanks to other three members with me Zizhao Han (PKU Physics), Dian Wu (PKU Physics) and Yixuan Wang (PKU Mathematics). We will represent PKU to participate the final competition in April 2019 @NYSE. Get the paper (in Chinease) here.

    *  (February 2018) I hold the hydrodynamics seminar at Room West 113 in school of physics. The activity time is 2:00-5:00 every Sunday afternoon. We cover a lot of topics including ideal fluid, viscous fluid and turbulence.

    *  (September 2017) I am happy to give a talk and discuss with students from Department of Mathematics in the numerical algebra seminar. My talk focues on numerical methods for QR-type and SVD-type decompositions.
